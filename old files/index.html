My name is Joshua Good and I am sophomore Computer Science major concentrating in Game Design studies.
While many would regard Game Design as simply the action of building a game, the true study of user
- system interaction extends far beyond the traditional elements of simple play. The art of Game Design
entails a deeper understanding of the complex systems that occur during the user's surface - level interaction
with game objects. Level design, audio engineering, programming simulations, and artificial intelligence/enemy
behaviors present different challenges that determine the atmosphere and function of a game. To a game designer,
the manner in which these elements appear to the player portrays the genre or play-style the player will experience
during gameplay. For example, traditional role playing game (RPG) mechanics center around the player's assumption
of a character that he or she must progress over the course of the adventure. Here, an effective environment must
encourage the player to explore and discover new locations, creating an immersive and believable world.

Expanding on my major, my favorite hobby is creating games. No matter the genre, games present a fascinating challenge
to solving multi-variable problems in addition to general player entertainment. While the core goal of a traditional game
tasks a design team to develop an entertaining and engaging experience, the method in which this goal is achieved depends
upon the game's intended direction. In my case, for programmers, this challenge becomes especially relevant as their job
consists of implementing different systems into the game environment while ensuring their intended functions. For instance,
if a design team wishes to immerse the player in an underwater environment, programmers must consider the movement of the
character, the interaction between the player character and randomly-encountered life, and the consideration of an oxygen
oxygen system if relevant to the design of the game. However, while I mostly develop traditional games, I have also developed
serious games, or games that teach a real-world concept to the player. When developing serious games, while creating educational
content can be entertaining, the greatest enjoyment comes with the satisfaction of students and other players saying that they learned
something new and want to see future content.

How to Make a Tuna Melt:

  1. Add a slice of Provolone or American cheese to a slice of bread
  2. Smear a thin layer of dijon or spicy brown mustard on the cheese
  3. Add tuna or tuna salad, depending on your preference
  4. Layer lettuce and tomato in that order on top of the tuna
  5. Grill on a skillet for approximately 5 - 10 minutes
  6. Enjoy!
